About ClearTable

Privacy

Audio enhancement is processed only on this phone. Nothing is recorded, saved, or sent anywhere. Tap Stop or close the tab and the microphone is released.

Live transcript uses your browser's built-in speech recognition. Depending on the browser, audio may be sent to Apple, Google, or another speech service to be turned into text. The transcript itself stays on this device and is cleared when you tap Clear or leave the page. Turn the transcript off if you don't want speech sent off-device.

Keep your phone awake

iPhones dim and lock after a short idle period, which can pause Safari and cut off the sound. ClearTable asks the browser to keep the screen awake while you're listening, using a small toggle near the status above. It also runs a tiny invisible looping video as a backup — a trick that helps on older iOS or in Low Power Mode.

If your iPhone still sleeps while testing, that's iOS blocking web keep-awake. The web cannot fully fix this. The only reliable workaround:

  1. Open Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock.
  2. Set it to Never while you're using ClearTable.
  3. Change it back afterwards.

Also: disable Low Power Mode, keep Safari in the foreground, and don't switch apps. A native iOS app would not have this restriction — this is a Safari/web-platform limitation, not a ClearTable bug.

Microphone source

For best results we want the iPhone's built-in microphone, with audio playing through your headset. Below the status you can see the active microphone and pick a different one if the browser offers more than one.

Web limitation: When a Bluetooth headset is connected, iOS Safari often forces the headset's microphone regardless of which device you select. This sounds thin and picks up your own voice, not the table. There is no reliable web workaround — a native iOS app is required to keep audio output on the headset while capturing from the iPhone mic.

Tips for restaurants
  • Place the phone face-up near the center of the table.
  • Keep it away from clinking glassware and cutlery.
  • If you don't hear anything, swipe into Control Center, long-press the audio tile, and pick your headset.
  • Keep Safari in the foreground — iOS may pause audio if you switch apps.
Sound isn't right
  • No sound? Check your headset is connected, then re-route via Control Center.
  • Tinny / call-quality sound? Some Bluetooth headsets switch to a low-quality call mode when the mic is on.
  • Choppy audio? Lower Smooth Noise Reduction one notch — the v5 engine already smooths cuts gently, but extreme settings can still pump.
  • Harsh or clipping? Lower Volume Boost. Auto level voices includes a soft limiter to keep peaks safe.
  • Far-away people too quiet? Turn Auto level voices on (default) and set Strength to High.
  • Hearing yourself loudly? Train and turn on Reduce my voice. This is best-effort and won't fully remove you.
Will it keep working when my phone sleeps?

Short answer: not reliably in this web prototype. Music apps keep playing with the screen off because iOS gives them a dedicated background audio mode that web pages don't have. Safari pauses or kills audio capture when the screen locks or you switch apps.

What works in the web version:

  • Set Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock to Never while listening.
  • Keep Safari in the foreground — don't switch apps.
  • Disable Low Power Mode.

A future native iOS app could request background audio so ClearTable keeps working with the screen off, just like a music app. That's a platform feature only native apps can use.

Auto level voices

Two stages working together: a real-time voice-band leveler that flattens loud peaks at the audio thread, and a slower automatic gain control (AGC) that lifts quieter, farther voices toward a steady listening level. A soft limiter behind both holds final peaks back to protect your hearing and the headset.

Strength Off applies only a transparent peak compressor and your manual Volume Boost. Light nudges levels gently. Medium behaves like a broadcast leveler. Strong (the default) is tuned for restaurant tables — it lifts farther voices substantially and pins louder near voices to roughly the same output level. The trade-off at Strong is that during quiet moments you may hear the room come up; that is the AGC making the most of every voice.

If a particular speaker still sounds too loud, the cause is usually that they are much closer to the phone than the others; try moving the phone slightly farther from them, or stepping the strength back to Medium.

Reduce my voice (best-effort)

Tap Train my voice, wait for the 3-second countdown, then read the prompt aloud at a normal voice and keep speaking until the timer ends (about 8 seconds). The button changes to Stop early if you finish sooner. ClearTable stores your average loudness and a rough spectral fingerprint on this device only — nothing is uploaded.

When the toggle is on, the app ducks the headset audio when it detects very loud, near-mic speech that broadly matches your profile. It eases back up after you stop talking.

Use Retrain my voice any time the room or your headset position changes a lot — a fresh profile is more reliable. The status next to the button tells you whether a profile is ready.

Honest limits: this is not voiceprint-based speaker separation. A web page cannot truly subtract one speaker from a single microphone. Expect partial reduction, occasional false trips on voices in your range, and momentary dips when you talk. A native iOS app with on-device ML (RNNoise / personal voice activity detection) would do this much better — the web platform does not have those primitives yet.
