About ClearTable

Privacy

Audio enhancement is processed only on this phone. Nothing is recorded, saved, or sent anywhere. Tap Stop or close the tab and the microphone is released.

Live transcript uses your browser's built-in speech recognition. Depending on the browser, audio may be sent to Apple, Google, or another speech service to be turned into text. The transcript itself stays on this device and is cleared when you tap Clear or leave the page. Turn the transcript off if you don't want speech sent off-device.

Keep your phone awake

iPhones dim and lock after a short idle period, which can pause Safari and cut off the sound. ClearTable asks the browser to keep the screen awake while you're listening, using a small toggle near the status above.

If your browser doesn't support that, or you turn it off, set Settings > Display & Brightness > Auto-Lock to Never while you're at the table, then change it back afterward. Keeping Safari in the foreground also helps.

Tips for restaurants
  • Place the phone face-up near the center of the table.
  • Keep it away from clinking glassware and cutlery.
  • If you don't hear anything, swipe into Control Center, long-press the audio tile, and pick your headset.
  • Keep Safari in the foreground — iOS may pause audio if you switch apps.
Sound isn't right
  • No sound? Check your headset is connected, then re-route via Control Center.
  • Tinny / call-quality sound? Some Bluetooth headsets switch to a low-quality call mode when the mic is on.
  • Choppy audio? Lower Noise Reduction one notch.
  • Harsh or clipping? Lower Volume Boost.
What it can and can't do
  • Expect 150–400 ms delay through Bluetooth — lip movement leads the sound.
  • Best for steady background noise and one or two nearby talkers.
  • Single-microphone classical EQ + dynamics. No AI denoiser, no beamforming.
  • Engine latency: —
Not a medical device

ClearTable is an experimental assistive listening prototype. It is not a hearing aid and does not diagnose, treat, or cure any condition. If you have hearing concerns, please see an audiologist. Use comfortable volumes only.
